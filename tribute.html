Martin Luther King Jr. (1929-1968)

If you can’t fly, run;
if you can’t run, walk;
if you can’t walk, crawl;
but by all means keep moving.
10.04.1960

A time line of Martin Luther King's life

  • 1929 - Michael King is born in Atlanta. His father changes the boy’s name, as well as his own, to Martin Luther King several years later
  • 1944 - King enrolls at Morehouse College after passing the entrance exam at age 15
  • 1946 - The Atlanta Constitution publishes a letter to the editor from King supporting minority rights
  • February 1948 - King is ordained and becomes assistant pastor at Ebenezer Baptist Church in Atlanta, his father’s church
  • June 1948 - King graduates from Morehouse College with bachelor’s degree in sociology
  • 1951 - King begins graduate studies in systematic theology at Boston University’s School of Theology
  • January 1952 - King meets Coretta Scott in Boston
  • 1953 -King and Coretta Scott are married near Marion, Ala. King’s father officiates at the service
  • 1954 - King begins his pastorate at Dexter Avenue Baptist Church in Montgomery, Alabama
  • 1955 - King earns doctorate from Boston University
  • 1957 - King appears on the cover of Time magazine
  • 1958 - At a book signing in Harlem, King is stabbed with a letter opener by a mentally ill woman. Doctors remove the seven-inch blade from his chest
  • 1960 - King is arrested at a sit-in demonstration at an Atlanta department store. He is sentenced to four months of hard labor
  • 1961 - King and hundreds of others are arrested in desegregation campaign in Albany, Georgia
  • 1962 - A member of the American Nazi Party hits King in the face twice at an SCLC conference in Birmingham
  • 28.08.1963 - King delivers his “I Have a Dream” speech at the Lincoln Memorial as more than 200,000 demonstrators take part in the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom
  • 1964 - King wins Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1965 - King gives his first speech against the Vietnam War
  • 1966 - King and his wife move into a Chicago slum apartment to demand better housing and education in northern U.S. cities
  • March 1968 -King leads 6,000 protesters in support of striking sanitation workers in Memphis
  • 03.04.1968 - King returns to Memphis, intending to lead a peaceful march. At an evening rally, he delivers his final speech, “I’ve Been to the Mountaintop.”
  • 04.04.1968 - King is shot and killed on the balcony of the Lorraine Motel
